How it works

Editing, writing and design work takes time and skill. Let me take that work off your hands.

Sorting through photos in a way that best tells the story is an art. So is writing just the right amount of copy. Don't end up with a jumble. Make your book the best it can be.

Photo sharing made simple

It can be puzzling

The easiest workflow is to collect your images in one Google Drive folder and share that folder with Storyline. If you would rather upload directly, there is space for that too.

How to prepare your photos

  1. 1

    Search your camera roll, desktop folders, text threads, and cloud albums for the pictures that belong in your project.

  2. 2

    Create a folder named for your book, like Smith Anniversary Book.

  3. 3

    Drop your favorites into that folder.

    • Rule of thumb: 150–350 photos per book (maximum 500)
    • It's fine if there are more photos than are used
    • Name photos by location or subject — e.g. Tokyo, Osaka — not default JPEG names
  4. 4

    Upload the folder to Google Drive.

  5. 5

    Share access with Storyline.

If you are unsure how to organize things, send what you have and give us a call. Storyline can help shape the photo selection from there.

Pricing basics

Clear and honest pricing

Storyline services

$4.50 per page with 20-page minimum plus tax, plus charges for photo book vendors (see below).

Printing & vendor costs

Printing, tax and shipping are paid to the photo book vendor (Shutterfly, Mixbook, Vistaprint, etc.). Storyline researches and recommends the best vendor for the project. Pricing varies by size, pages and specs. Vendor discounts are applied.

When payment is due

Payment is due upon placement of order, based on the estimated number of pages and book specs. An estimate is supplied before work begins, and adjustments can be made if more pages are added.

Accepted payments

ACH, Venmo, cash and Zelle preferred. We also accept Visa and Mastercard.
